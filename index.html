Marie Curie

The first person and the only woman to win a Nobel Prize twice

Timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw
  • 1891 - Moves to France
  • 1895 - Married Pierre Curie
  • 1897 - Gave birth to her first daughter
  • 1898 - Discovered two important radioactive elements called Polonium and Radium
  • 1903 - Took her first Nobel Prize. This was Marie's First Nobel Prize. This was awarded to Henri Becquerel, Pierre Curie, and Marie. This was the Physics Nobel prize, and was awarded for opening an entire new field of physics.
  • 1904 - Gave birth to her second daughter
  • 1906 - Her husband is killed by a horse drawn vehicle.
  • 1911 - Marie won her second Nobel prize, this time in chemistry, for her discovery of radium and polonium. This made her the first, and so far the only person to have won the Nobel Prize in two different categories.
  • 1934 - Dies because of the long term exposure to radiation
